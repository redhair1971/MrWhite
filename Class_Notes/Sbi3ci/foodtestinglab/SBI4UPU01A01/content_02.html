Organic Chemistry

Organic chemistry is sometimes called carbon chemistry since organic molecules form from a carbon frame.  Carbon can form four bonds from a single atom and has a unique ability to make long chains with itself.  This is called catenation.  Carbon molecules can make long chains as well as chains that branch. 

When carbon is bonded to hydrogen alone, the type of compound formed is called a hydrocarbon.  Hydrocarbons are organic molecules.


Ethane – linear hydrocarbon
Ethylene – linear hydrocarbon
with a triple bond
Isobutane – branched hydrocarbon

Functional groups can be attached to hydrocarbons. These functional groups change the reactivity, function, and characteristics of the hydrocarbon.
