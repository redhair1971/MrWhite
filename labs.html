General Method for Writing a Lab Report

Informal

  1. Title- Make sure it is very descriptive
  2. Purpose- A purpose provides a question that you feel needs to answered, an educated guess as to what you think may happen, and reasons for why you would be doing the experiment
  3. Results- Complete all necessary recordings of data.  Provide an informative way of presentation: Graphs, Table, etc
  4. Discussion- Answer the questions completely in the discussion section of the lab sheet.

Formal

  1. Title- Make sure it is very descriptive
  2. Introduction-This is where information that is already known about the subject is placed.  It includes past reseach and a description of the subject that is being used in the experiment
  3. Purpose- A purpose provides a question that you feel needs to answered, an educated guess as to what you think may happen, and reasons for why you would be doing the experiment
  4. Materials- Please list the materials you used (from the lab sheet)
  5. Procedure- Reference the lab sheet and make sure to include any changes in the procedure.
  6. Observation- Includes the data you collected and any additional information you felt was important when the lab started or during the experiment.
  7. Results- Complete all tables and necessary graphs.  All calculations should be as neat and complete.  A ruler is important to provide neatness.
  8. Discussion- Answer the questions completely in the discussion section of the lab sheet.
  9. Conclusion- Answer the questions completely in the conclusion section of the lab sheet.

 

Design your own experiment

Write a formal lab report for an experiment. The experiment is thought out and planned but it does not need to be performed. It will use fictitious data that you provide to mimic a real experiment.

Purpose

This assignment will demonstrate the understanding of the components of a standard formal lab report as well as the understanding of the different types of variables involved in an experiment.

Materials

2-3 sheets of paper and your imagination

Method

Your formal lab report should contain the following:

A. Problem - A short description of the reasoning behind the selection of your topic as well as the question you are trying to answer.

B. Hypothesis - Try to use "If…then…" statements but make sure you are giving a prediction of how the questioned will be answered.

C. Materials - Short list of the stuff you think you will need.

D. Procedure - Describe how the experiment would have been performed. Identify the independent, dependent and two controlled variables.

E. Observations (Results) - Provide a table/graph for your results. Provide a qualitative statement and a quantitative statement. (Fully label, use a ruler, provide a title)

F. Conclusion - Compare your results with your problem and your hypothesis. Provide the most important finding.

G. Sources of Error - Describe two sources of error that may affect your experiment and general human error does not count

H. Discussion - Where can you see this type of research going? Provide the next logical step; identify what experiment you would perform.

Remember this is to show understanding of a formal lab report. Pen or typed reports only.
